Anchor Info

"And beginning with Moses and all the Prophets, he explained to them what was said in all the Scriptures concerning himself."

— Luke 24:27
01

Made for Discipleship

For centuries, Jewish culture placed great value on storytelling and the faithful passing down of truth through oral tradition. Each generation carefully retold the story of God and His people so that the next would know not only the events, but the meaning behind them. Anchor's objective is to make use of this tool of the Sages. It has been designed with discipleship in mind, to help new and old Christians grasp the big picture narrative of Scripture through conversation, guided discovery, and shared reflection. Anchor aids believers to see how every part of the Bible connects to the greater story of redemption. It is a gospel tool to walk through Scripture, teaching, discussing, and learning along the way.

02

Made for Devotionals

Trying to study the Bible without understanding how the whole story fits together is a bit like starting a movie halfway through. It can feel confusing, difficult to follow, and even intimidating. But once you begin to see the overarching story of Scripture, everything changes. You'll discover the greatest story ever told and it includes you. Suddenly, the pieces begin to fall into place. You become eager to explore the parts you haven’t yet read and excited to revisit the moments you love most. Anchor was created to help build that big-picture understanding of the Bible in a way that is engaging, interactive, and memorable. If you’re unsure where to begin in your devotional time, choose a card you’re less familiar with and fill in the missing pieces. Slowly, you’ll begin filling in the picture of God’s unfolding plan.

03

Made for Community

Anchor is not only a personal tool—it is designed to be shared. Use it with friends, family, or your church community. Challenge one another, test your knowledge, and grow together in your understanding of Scripture’s grand narrative. See who can arrange the story most accurately. Talk through the events. Discuss how God has worked throughout history and how His promises unfold from beginning to end. In doing so, you’ll not only strengthen your knowledge of the Bible—you’ll deepen relationships and build meaningful conversations around God’s Word in a way that is both fun and interactive.
