Bible Study

Anchor isn't an interpretive resource or commentary. Rather it's a Gospel tool designed to spark curiosity, and help you find your place in the greatest story ever told.

Discovery

The Bible can be explored in many ways, One incredible way is through curiosity and discovery! Often, ending in profound spiritual growth and a desire to find out even more. How can you use Anchor for guided discovery?

Browse the Anchor cards and pick one that:

  • You've never encountered before and want to explore.
  • Catches your eye and looks interesting.
  • You once knew but want to revisit with fresh eyes.
✨ Remember: Some of the most awe-inspiring revelations come not from a specific study plan, but reliance on the Holy Spirit and an open Bible.

Biblical Literacy

When we understand the context of the Bible passage we are reading, we allow the Bible to speak for itself rather than reading our own ideas into it. That is why Anchor is grounded in solid academic foundations and designed to be explored modularly, one piece at a time, in the order that serves you best.

  • 1
    Go chronologically: Start at the beginning of the Old or New Testament and work through it in order.
  • 2
    Explore a theme: Choose a theme like Kingship, Return, or Passion Week and understand our that theme shapes the redemptive plan
  • 3
    Practice the order: Shuffle your cards regularly, then put them back in sequence. Doing this alongside your reading builds a mental story-map of the Bible that sticks.

Launch Point

Anchor covers a huge amount of the Old Testament and the entirety of the New.But you don't have to follow the rules perfectly. Let your curiosity lead.

  • Pick any card and explore how it fits into God's bigger plan. If reading one passage pulls you somewhere else off script, go there. That's exactly the point, to anchor yourself in the Bible and keep reading.
  • If your devotional momentum stalls. Shuffle the deck, find a new starting point, and get your head back in the Word.
📖 Use Anchor as a tool, not a rule. It exists to serve your journey into the Bible, not to define its boundaries. Go off-script whenever the Holy Spirit leads you there.
