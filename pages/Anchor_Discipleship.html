Discipleship Guide

Anchor is a gospel tool designed to help you walk alongside others, through honest conversation, shared curiosity, and guided discovery through Scripture.

You Don't Have to Know It All

One of the biggest barriers to discipleship is the belief that you need to have all the answers first. You don't... and you never will. Disciples are meant to make disciples (Matt 28:16–20). Using Anchor can help you know where to start.

  • Start with a simple invitation. Reach out to a friend, family member, or someone in your life who could use a little guided discovery. Pray beforehand and trust that God will provide what's needed in the moment.
  • Keep it relaxed and low-pressure. Play a game of Anchor over coffee, hot chocolate, or whatever's on the table. Anchor is designed so that even someone with little Bible knowledge can engage fully, the colour system makes it accessible from the very first card.
  • Let the conversation happen naturally. As you play together, questions and moments of curiosity will surface on their own. Share what you know. Speak into what you notice. And when you don't know the answer, say so, "I'm not sure" is always an honest and welcome response.

Discipling Through Themes

One of the most powerful ways to help someone understand the Bible is to show them how a single thread runs through the entire story. Choose a theme you're familiar with and walk through it together.

  • 1
    Pick a theme you know. Lay out the relevant cards and walk through how that theme unfolds. Let the cards do the heavy lifting.
  • 2
    Share what it's meant to you. Theology hits different when it's personal. Tell them not just what a theme or even card means, but how understanding it has shaped the way you read the Bible or live your faith.
  • 3
    Invite them to go deeper. After exploring together, ask what they noticed. Let them go and learn more about it and then discuss it the next time you meet.

The world is facing a discipleship crisis and you are part of the answer. You don't need to be a theologian. You need to be willing. Pick up the cards and start the conversation.
